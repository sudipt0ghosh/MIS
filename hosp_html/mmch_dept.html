LIST OF DEPARTMENT




Anaesthesiology

anaesthesia or anaesthetics (see Terminology) is the medical speciality concerned with the total perioperative care of patients before, during and after surgery.





General surgery

is a surgical specialty that focuses on abdominal contents including esophagus, stomach, small bowel, colon, liver and etc.






ENT

Otorhinolaryngologists are surgical specialists who diagnose, evaluate and manage a wide range of diseases of the head and neck, including the ear, nose and throat regions.




Dentistry

is a branch of medicine that consists of the study, diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of diseases, disorders, and conditions of the oral cavity.






Forensic toxicology

is the use of toxicology and other disciplines such as analytical chemistry, pharmacology and clinical chemistry to aid medical or legal investigation of death, poisoning, and drug use.




Dermatology

is the branch of medicine dealing with the skin, nails, hair and its diseases. It is a specialty with both medical and surgical aspects




community medicine

a branch of medicine dealing with health care issues affecting communities as a whole







Anatomy

is the study of the structure of an object, in this case the human body. Human anatomy deals with the way the parts of humans, from molecules to bones, interact to form a functional unit.




Respiratory medicine

:-Pulmonology is a medical speciality that deals with diseases involving the respiratory tract. The term is derived from the Latin word pulmō, pulmōnis and the Greek suffix -λογία, -logia. Pulmonology is synonymous with pneumology, respirology and respiratory medicine
